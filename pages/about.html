About Me

Introduction

Strange for me to be creating a Developer Portfolio when in fact I am not a developer. Yet. Over the last two years I have taken up coding as a hobby. I have done several courses on the Coursera platform, most notably a Python Specialisation which culminated in a Capstone project. Building on my knowledge of Python, I built a Blog Application using the Django framework, by following a YouTube tutorial. Another hobby I am passionate about, and spend a lot of resources on, is learning to speak Polish. The Django blog application came in handy as a tool for learning Polish; I use it as a notebook I can access on my phone. I also did a few courses on the Udemy platform, mostly HTML, Sass-CSS and JavaScript. It is really great following tutorials, but itâ€™s a bit like paint by numbers. You are following someone who has some mastery over their art. You are not witness to the sweat and tears it took to become proficient. You are witnessing the finished product. That is what brings me to Boolean. A willingness to commit and work hard, so I too can become a master of this fine art. Coding.

Industrial Chemist

What has Industrial Chemistry got to do with coding. Well, everything and nothing. After leaving High School I studied Industrial Chemistry for five years. Are the skills acquired as an Industrial Chemist transferable into the world of coding. I worked as an Industrial Chemist for 12 years, and yes, the skills acquired are transferrable to, life in general, and the discipline of coding.

Tibetan Buddhist Monk

12 years of Industrial Chemistry was enough for 1 life-time. Time for a radical change. In 2003 I came to the UK to participate in a four-year-cloistered meditation retreat held by Samye Ling Monastery in Eskdalemuir, Scotland. I studied Tibetan Buddhist Meditation under some very accomplished Tibetan Masters. One four-year retreat was not enough, so after a years break I applied for a Contemplative Scholarship through the Tsadra Foundation, which I was granted, and entered and completed a second four-year-cloistered retreat. On leaving the retreat I met my partner, who happens to be Polish, hence my interest in learning the Polish Language.

Complex Clinical Care
